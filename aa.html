Q1: What is Software Project Management? Describe the four important phases of the Project Management Life Cycle. Software Project Management is the discipline of planning, executing, monitoring, and completing software development projects. It involves applying management principles to software projects to deliver high-quality software within time and budget constraints.

Key responsibilities include:
- Planning scope, schedule, and resources.
- Managing risks and quality.
- Coordinating team efforts.
- Monitoring progress and adapting to changes.

Four Important Phases of the Project Management Life Cycle:
1. Initiation Phase:
- Define project goals, objectives, and scope.
- Conduct feasibility studies.
- Identify stakeholders and get approvals.
- Prepare a project charter.
2. Planning Phase:
- Develop a detailed project plan (tasks, timeline, resources).
- Identify risks and prepare mitigation strategies.
- Set budget estimates and schedules.
- Assign responsibilities and communication plans.
3. Execution Phase:
- Implement the project plan.
- Allocate resources and manage the development process.
- Track team performance and maintain communication.
- Ensure quality and manage stakeholder expectations.
4. Closure Phase:
- Finalize project deliverables and get stakeholder approval.
- Close contracts and release resources.
- Document lessons learned.
- Conduct post-project evaluation.
